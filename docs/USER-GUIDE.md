# Cadence — Step-by-Step User Guide

*One board for every client.*

This guide walks through Cadence from first launch to publishing a post, covering
every feature along the way. If you're new, follow it top to bottom — it's ordered
the way you'd actually use the app.

---

## 1. Getting oriented

### Signing in
Cadence is protected by a shared **workspace password**. Enter it once and you stay
signed in on that device for 7 days; sign out with the exit icon at the far right of
the top bar. Clients never sign in — see [Section 9](#9-the-client-approval-flow).

When you open Cadence you land on **the board** — your calendar. Three regions:

| Region | What it's for |
| --- | --- |
| **Top bar** | Date navigation, view switcher, density, theme, Ideas, New post |
| **Left rail** | Client list + filters (brand, channel, status) |
| **Main area** | The current view (Month, Week, List, Board, or Feed) |

Cadence **remembers your last view**, density, theme, and filters between sessions,
so the board opens the way you left it.

> **First time, with no clients yet?** You'll see a welcome screen instead of the
> calendar. Click **Add your first client** and jump to [Section 8](#8-managing-clients-channels-and-recurring-slots).

---

## 2. Choosing how you see your work (the 5 views)

Use the view switcher in the top bar, or press number keys `1`–`5`.

### Month — press `1`
The default. A full month grid, one box per day, posts shown as compact colored chips.

- Each chip shows a **brand dot** (client color), the **post title**, an **approval
  icon** if relevant, and the **channel icon**.
- Chip background color = **status** (see the status legend in the rail).
- If a day has more posts than fit, a **"+N more"** link opens that day in a popup.
- **Hover any day** and a small `+` appears in the corner — click it to create a
  post on that date.

### Week — press `2`
A time-gridded week, 06:00–21:00, so you can see *when* in the day things post.

- Posts sit in their exact hour row.
- **Double-click any empty cell** to create a post at that day and time.

### List — press `3`
A dense chronological list grouped by day ("Today", "Tomorrow", then dates). Best for
bulk work and for scanning a lot of posts fast. See [Section 6](#6-bulk-actions-list-view).

### Board — press `4`
A kanban board with one column per status: **Idea → Draft → Needs approval → Approved
→ Scheduled → Published**. Drag cards between columns to move work forward.

### Feed — press `5`
A **channel preview** — how the content will actually look in the feed.

- Pick a **client** (colored chips along the top), then a **channel**.
- Instagram and TikTok render as a **3-column grid** so you can check the visual
  rhythm of the profile before anything goes out. Upcoming posts carry a small date badge.
- YouTube and Facebook render as wide video/link cards.
- X and LinkedIn render as a text feed with the real copy.

## 3. Switching between one client and all clients

At the top of the left rail is the **All clients / Single client** toggle. This is
the core of Cadence.

- **All clients** — every brand on one calendar, color-coded. This is the oversight
  view: your whole week across every account at a glance. Click any client name in
  the rail to show/hide just that brand (click several to compare a subset). Click
  **show all** to reset.
- **Single client** — focus mode. Click a client in the rail and the board shows only
  that brand.

### Filtering
Below the client list:

- **Channels** — the six platform icons (Instagram, TikTok, X, LinkedIn, Facebook,
  YouTube). Click to toggle. Dimmed = hidden.
- **Status** — click any status to toggle it. Useful combo: filter to **Needs approval**
  in All-clients mode to see everything waiting on a client, across every account.

Each client row shows a **post count** so you can spot an account that's gone quiet.

## 4. Creating a post (the Composer)

Click **+ New post**, press `N`, hover-click the `+` on a day, or double-click a
week cell.

The Composer asks for:

1. **Client** — which brand this belongs to.
2. **Date & time** — pre-filled if you started from a calendar cell.
3. **Content type** — image, video, carousel, text, reel, story, or short.
4. **Working title** — how the post shows on the calendar (e.g. "August launch teaser").
5. **Channels** — only the channels that client actually has connected appear here.
   Pick one or several.
6. **Base copy** — written once and applied to every channel you selected. You tailor
   it per platform in the next step. If X is selected you get a live **280-character
   counter**.
7. **Tile** — an emoji that stands in for the media thumbnail on the calendar.

Click **Create draft**. The post lands on the calendar as a Draft and the detail
drawer opens automatically so you can refine it.

---

## 5. Editing a post (the detail drawer)

Click any post card, anywhere, to open the drawer on the right.

### Per-channel variants
This is the piece that saves the most time. One idea, tailored per platform:

- The **Channels** row toggles which platforms this post goes to.
- Below it, a **tab per channel**. Each tab has its own copy box.
- Every tab shows a **live character counter** against that platform's real limit
  (X 280, Instagram 2,200, LinkedIn 3,000, Facebook 5,000, YouTube 5,000). Go over and
  the counter turns **red and bold**.

Edit the title or any copy and a **Save changes** button appears. Status, content
type, and schedule changes save immediately.

### Moving a post through the workflow
The drawer's action row adapts to where the post is:

- **Request approval** — sets status to *Needs approval* and marks it awaiting the client.
- **Mark approved** — approve it yourself, without waiting on the client.
- **Schedule** — move an approved post to *Scheduled*.
- **Publish now** — pushes it live through the publishing service and flips it to
  *Published*.
- **Copy client link** — copies that client's shareable approval link.
- **Trash icon** — delete the post.

### Comments and @mentions
At the bottom, leave a comment for your team or the client. Type `@name` and the
mention renders highlighted. **Client comments are tinted amber** with a "client"
badge, so feedback from outside never gets lost in internal chatter.

---

## 6. Bulk actions (List view)

Switch to **List** (`3`) when you need to change many posts at once.

1. Tick the checkbox on each post, or use the **select-all** box in the header.
2. A **bulk action bar** slides up from the bottom:
   - **Set status…** — move everything to any status at once.
   - **+1 day / +1 week / −1 day** — shift every selected post's schedule. Ideal when
     a client's launch slips a week.
   - **Delete** — removes them (asks for confirmation first).
   - **Clear** — deselect everything.

---

## 7. Rescheduling by dragging

Drag and drop works in three places:

- **Month** — drag a post to a different day. The time of day is preserved.
- **Week** — drag to any day *and hour* cell to change both date and time.
- **Board** — drag between status columns to advance the workflow. Dropping into
  *Needs approval* automatically flags it as awaiting the client.

Drop targets highlight as you drag over them.

---

## 8. Managing clients, channels, and recurring slots

Click **manage** next to "Clients" in the rail. For each client you get:

### Connected channels
The channel icons show which platforms that brand is set up on.

### The client approval link
Each client has a **shareable review link**. Click **Copy approval link** and send it
to your client — see [Section 9](#9-the-client-approval-flow).

### Recurring slots
For the posts that happen on a rhythm — "post every Tuesday at 9am."

- Click **+ add slot**, then set a **label**, **weekday**, **hour**, and **channel**.
- Once slots exist, click **Fill next 2 weeks**. Cadence creates draft posts on every
  matching slot for the next fortnight, so your recurring commitments are already on
  the calendar waiting for copy.
- This is safe to click repeatedly — it **skips slots that already have a post**, so
  you'll never get duplicates.

### Adding a new client
Click **+ Add a client** and set:

- **Name** and **industry**
- **Accent color** — pick from eight. This is the client's color everywhere on the
  calendar, so choose distinct colors for accounts you compare often.
- **Channels** — which platforms they're on.

## 9. The client approval flow

The whole point: **your client does not need an account.**

**On your side:**
1. Set a post to **Needs approval** (via the drawer or by dragging it into that
   column on the Board).
2. Copy the client's approval link (from the drawer, or from **manage**).
3. Send them the link.

**On their side**, they see a clean review page with:
- A **"Waiting for your review"** section listing only what needs a decision.
- The **actual copy for every channel**, so they see exactly what will post where.
- A name field (remembered for next time) so their comments are attributed.
- **Approve** or **Request changes** on each post. Requesting changes prompts them
  to say what should change.
- A **"Coming up"** section showing what's already approved and scheduled.

**Back on your board**, the result appears immediately:
- **Approved** → status moves to *Approved*, green check on the card.
- **Request changes** → status returns to *Draft*, the card is flagged, and their
  note lands in the post's comments in amber.

> **Privacy note:** the review page only ever exposes posts that are *needs approval*,
> *approved*, or *scheduled* — your ideas and rough drafts stay private. A client's
> link is scoped strictly to their own brand.

## 10. The Ideas button (AI idea generator)

When a client's calendar has gaps, click **💡 Ideas** in the top bar.

1. **Pick the client.** Suggestions are shaped by that brand's industry and its
   connected channels.
2. Click **Generate ideas**. You get six suggestions, each with:
   - A **topic/title** — the actual post concept.
   - A **hook** — the reasoning: why this angle works and how to execute it.
   - A suggested **content type** and **channels**.
3. Click **Draft** on any idea you like. It drops straight onto the calendar as a
   **draft card** on an upcoming morning, ready for you to write. The button changes
   to **Added ✓**.
4. Click **Generate ideas** again for a fresh set of angles — you can keep pulling
   until something lands.

Nothing is committed until you click Draft, so it's safe to browse.

## 11. Making the board readable

- **Density** — the toggle in the top bar (or press `D`) switches **compact**
  (maximum posts visible, titles lead each chip) and **comfortable** (larger cards
  with media thumbnails, copy previews, and post times).
- **Theme** — the moon/sun icon toggles **light and dark**. Your choice is remembered
  and applied before the page paints, so there's no flash.
- **Collapse the rail** — the panel icon at the far left hides the sidebar to give the
  calendar full width. Worth doing on a busy month, since wider cells show longer titles.

## 12. Keyboard shortcuts

Press `?` at any time for this list in-app.

| Key | Action |
| --- | --- |
| `N` | New post |
| `T` | Jump to today |
| `1` – `5` | Month / Week / List / Board / Feed |
| `←` `→` (or `[` `]`) | Previous / next period |
| `D` | Toggle density |
| `Esc` | Close any open panel or drawer |
| `?` | Show shortcuts |

Shortcuts are ignored while you're typing in a field, so they never interrupt writing copy.

---

## 13. On phones and tablets

Cadence is fully responsive:

- Small screens **default to List view** rather than a cramped month grid.
- The filter rail becomes a **bottom sheet** — tap the menu icon in the top bar.
- Cards, drawers, and the client review page all reflow to a single column.

Send clients the approval link with confidence — the review page is built to be used
from a phone.

## A typical week in Cadence

1. **Monday morning** — open on **All clients / Month** to see the week across every account.
2. Filter to **Needs approval** to catch anything a client hasn't answered yet; nudge them.
3. Hit **Ideas** for any client looking thin, and drop 2–3 drafts onto the calendar.
4. Open each draft, write the **per-channel variants**, and check the character counters.
5. **Request approval** and send each client their link.
6. As approvals land, drag cards to **Scheduled** on the Board.
7. Use **Feed** view to sanity-check how each client's grid will look before it goes out.

## Note on the current build

Two services are abstracted behind swappable interfaces and currently run as
simulations, so you can exercise the full workflow end to end:

- **Publishing** — "Publish now" runs through the publishing service and marks the
  post published, but does not yet post to the live platform APIs.
- **Idea generation** — suggestions come from a built-in angle library rather than a
  live AI model.

Both are single-implementation swaps to go live; everything else — the data model,
approvals, scheduling, comments, and all views — is real and persistent.
